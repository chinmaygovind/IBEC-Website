Portfolio Governance

Investment Committee

The Investment Committee (IC) is the decision-making body responsible for managing the IBEC Penn Fund. Composed of members appointed by the Executive Board, the IC reviews research and recommendations from regional committees, votes on portfolio changes, and ensures adherence to the Fund's Investment Policy Statement. Through disciplined governance and collaborative analysis, the IC provides members with hands-on experience in institutional-quality investment management.

How the IC Operates

Three interconnected roles drive the Fund's investment process from research to execution.

⚖

Executive Board

Determines the composition of the Investment Committee, appoints and manages IC members, and oversees the strategic direction of the Fund.

⚙

Investment Committee

Holds decision rights for all portfolio changes, reviews recommendations, votes on trade approvals, and monitors compliance with the IPS.

★

Regional Committees

Develop in-depth research and security recommendations within their respective geographic regions, feeding ideas up to the IC for review.

1

Investment Objective

The Fund targets high total returns through a diversified public-markets portfolio while giving members practical experience in global macro awareness, security selection, portfolio construction, and risk management.

Equities

Publicly traded common stocks, ADRs, and equity ETFs across all market capitalizations.

Fixed Income

U.S. Treasuries and agencies, investment-grade corporate bonds, and sovereign bonds of developed and select emerging markets.

Alternatives

Liquid, exchange-traded exposures such as listed REITs, commodity ETFs, listed infrastructure, and listed private equity vehicles.

Cash & Equivalents

U.S. Treasury bills, government money-market funds, and similar high-liquidity instruments.

2

Strategic Asset Allocation

The Fund maintains target allocations with defined bands. If an allocation breaches its permitted range, the IC rebalances back within bounds.

Equities60%55% – 65%

3

Investment Process

From idea generation to ongoing monitoring, every step of the process is designed to mirror institutional best practices.

Idea Generation

Investment ideas originate from regional committees or any IBEC member and are submitted for IC review.

Review & Vote

The IC discusses thesis quality, portfolio fit, and risk impact. Proposals are approved by a simple majority of voting members when a quorum is present.

Execution & Recording

Approved trades are executed, recorded in the IBEC fund trade log, and reflected in performance and risk reporting.

Monitoring & Exit Discipline

Positions are reviewed regularly and may be reduced or exited if the thesis breaks, valuation reaches target, or a position causes guideline breaches.
