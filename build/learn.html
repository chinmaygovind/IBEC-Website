§ Foundations Program Six weeks · Self-paced New member track

Learn the language.

A six-week curriculum for new WIBEC apprentices. No finance background required — just the commitment to work through it and arrive at committee meetings ready to contribute.

§ 01 — Curriculum

Six weeks.

The program runs in parallel with the first six weeks of the apprenticeship. Each module takes 3–4 hours and includes readings, a short written response, and one discussion prompt for committee.

Week 01

How markets actually work.

Price discovery, liquidity, why markets exist, and what they do (and don't) do well. Focus on equity markets, with brief coverage of FX and fixed income.

Readings
  • The Intelligent Investor — Preface
  • Malkiel: "A Random Walk" Ch. 1–2
  • FT Guide to Equity Markets, pp. 1–28
Week 02

Reading macro data.

GDP, CPI, unemployment, PMI, retail sales — what they measure, why they matter, and how markets react to each. Practice reading an actual data release.

Readings
  • BLS: How to read a CPI release
  • Fed Primer: Understanding Unemployment
  • Bloomberg Opinion: "What PMI Actually Tells Us"
Week 03

Valuation basics.

P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B, and when each matters. Discounted cash flow — what it is and what it can't tell you. The relationship between rates and multiples.

Readings
  • Damodaran: "Valuation for Dummies" (excerpt)
  • WIBEC internal: "When multiples mislead"
Week 04

Fixed income and FX.

Yield curves, sovereign spreads, carry trades. How currency dynamics interact with equity returns in international portfolios. Emerging market risk premia.

Readings
  • BIS: "What Moves EM FX?" (2024)
  • FT: "Reading the yield curve — a practical guide"
Week 05

Writing a thesis.

What makes a good investment thesis vs. a description. Variant perception — why you need to know what the consensus believes before you can disagree with it.

Readings
  • WIBEC internal: "Anatomy of a pitch memo"
  • Howard Marks: "The Most Important Thing" Ch. 4
Week 06

Your first pitch.

Working session with your committee lead. Draft a one-page thesis on any security or macro theme. Feedback from the lead before you present to committee.

Deliverable

One-page pitch memo.
Submitted to committee lead
by end of week six.
